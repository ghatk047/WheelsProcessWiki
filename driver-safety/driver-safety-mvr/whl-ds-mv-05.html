WHL-DS-MV-05

High-Risk Driver Intervention and Coaching Workflow

🏢 Driver Safety & Compliance ➡ Driver Safety & MVR Programme 📝 10 L4 steps ✅ Complete
Description
The High-Risk Driver Intervention and Coaching Workflow is designed to identify drivers at risk based on MVR data and telematics scoring. It involves a series of actions to intervene, coach, and monitor these drivers to improve safety and compliance.
Trigger
Triggered when a driver's MVR report or telematics score indicates high-risk behavior (e.g., speeding, multiple accidents, violations).
Outcome
Successful completion ensures that high-risk drivers are identified, coached, and their behaviors improved to reduce accident rates and enhance overall safety.

🎯 KPIs & Performance Targets
  • Vehicle uptime > 95%
  • Invoice accuracy > 99%
  • MVR accuracy > 98%
  • Alert response time < 24 hours
  • Resource allocation time < 3 working days
  • Coaching session attendance rate > 85%
  • Confirmation receipt rate > 90%
  • Action plan completion rate > 80%
  • Action plan monitoring efficiency > 75%
  • Follow-up alert issuance rate < 30%
  • Driver safety improvement rate > 70%
⚠️ Operational Risks
  • Inaccurate MVR data affecting timely intervention.
  • Slow response time leading to delayed driver coaching.
  • Delays in resource allocation affecting driver intervention.
  • Low attendance rates due to scheduling conflicts or driver reluctance.
  • Drivers not receiving or responding to confirmation notifications.
  • Incomplete or ineffective action plans reducing driver improvement.
  • Lack of visibility into action plan progress affecting driver improvement.
  • Inefficient resource reallocation leading to prolonged intervention times.
  • High follow-up alerts indicating ineffective driver intervention.
  • Low driver safety improvement rates affecting fleet safety metrics.
